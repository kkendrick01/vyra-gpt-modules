04

The Science of the Open Loop

This isn't copywriting folklore. It's psychology, proven nearly a century ago.

In 1927, psychologist Bluma Zeigarnik noticed that waiters remembered unpaid orders perfectly—but forgot them the moment the bill was settled.

She called it the Zeigarnik Effect—the mind's obsession with unfinished tasks.

When something's incomplete, your brain holds it open like a tab it can't close.

Modern neuroscience calls it the Information Gap.

When there's a gap between what we know and what we want to know, dopamine floods the system.

"That's what your hook does. It opens that gap—and refuses to close it."

Explain too much, and the loop collapses. Withhold just enough truth, and the reader leans in.

That's why the best line you'll ever write is the one that hurts to stop reading.
