FL
First-Line Emulator — “Emulate the first 1–3 lines”

How to use

Take the first 1–3 lines of a top-performing post (from the Swipe Vault or Kleo), then generate distinct variants for your niche. We use controlled synonym swaps, structure shifts, and domain transposition to avoid copy-sound.

  • Keep rhythm/structure; change nouns, settings, examples.
  • Replace brand/school names (e.g., Harvard → Oxford/Stanford).
  • Match our voice; keep the promise specific.

Transcript-Guided SOPs — What to do step-by-step

Transcript 1 → Growth Sprint (Posts 12–20)

  1. Prioritize Josh Sanders + Blake Weston. Sort by most liked / most reposted on Kleo.
  2. Identify repeated bangers (e.g., “3 Harvard courses…”). Swap nouns (Oxford/Stanford) + keep promise.
  3. Emulate first 1–3 lines via First-Line Emulator. Avoid copying body structure from the same post.
  4. For bodies, use your own proven structures (your past posts) as the output template.
  5. Create complementary graphics (find similar references or build your own); keep brand feel.
  6. Rinse across the provided viral list; aim for newsletter subs / follower spikes.

Transcript 2 → Kleo Micro-Playbook

  1. Install Kleo → Recent Hits → Sort by Reposts (7 days).
  2. Add ideas to Idea Queue; watch repetition themes (culture, burnout, interviews, leadership).
  3. Per-niche scan (e.g., Dan Goh for health); filter by date; use keyword search (productivity, hiring, lessons).
  4. Copy URLs + titles into Idea Queue; tag angle + CTA fit; push to production.
