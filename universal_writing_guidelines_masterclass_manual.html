1) Operating Principles (the “why” you’ll follow forever)

1.1 Write for Scanners

Most people don’t read— they scan. Scannable structure, concise phrasing, and objective language dramatically increase usability and engagement [1].

Make it scannable

  • Paragraphs ≤ 3 lines
  • Front-load key words in headings & first lines
  • Use bullets for steps, proof, and takeaways
  • Break long sequences with mini-headers
  • Add “signposts”: Key Insight, So What, Do This Next

Example (scan-first)

Key Insight: People share specifics, not slogans.

  • Numbers & names beat adjectives.
  • Short lines keep momentum.
  • One promise per paragraph.

1.2 Design for Fluency

Processing fluency (ease) makes information feel truer, safer, and more persuasive [2]. Your job is to remove friction.

❌ Low-fluency: “We endeavor to facilitate synergistic content ecosystems…”

✅ High-fluency: “We build content that grows revenue.”

  • Short, familiar words; strong verbs; consistent tense
  • Predictable structure (no surprise detours)
  • Whitespace and repetition to reduce cognitive load
  • Read-aloud test: if you stumble, so will the reader

1.3 Persuade with Proof

Build arguments and CTAs with authority, social proof, clarity of benefit, and ethical urgency [3].

Proof menu

  • Client count, named logos (with permission)
  • Before/after metrics (timeframe, % change)
  • Press, awards, certifications
  • Credible third-party citations

Drop-in line

“Used by 132 founders to add 25–40% more pipeline in 8 weeks.”

1.4 Rhythm is a Weapon (Gary Provost “Write Music”)

Cadence controls attention. Vary sentence length to create flow and emphasis. Gary Provost’s famous passage demonstrates how short and long sentences create “music.” Example excerpt (≤25 words): “This sentence has five words. Here are five more words. Five-word sentences are fine.” Use this principle—don’t copy the full text.

Practical rule: Cluster short lines for punch. Follow with one longer, rolling sentence to carry nuance—then cut back to a sharp “stab” line.

1.5 Specific & Concrete > Clever

Concrete details beat vague claims in headlines and body copy; clarity increases clicks when baseline vagueness is high [4][5].

❌ “Reinvent your growth engine today.”

✅ “Add 10–15 qualified demos/week with a 9-step outbound playbook.”

2) The Elite Writing System (end-to-end)

A) Hooks that Earn Attention

Goal: Make continued reading feel inevitable.

Inputs

  • X: X Pro + your swipe columns for proven hook skeletons
  • LinkedIn: Kleo “recent hits” for high-performing openers

Hook Scorecard (pass only if all true)

  • Specific payoff is explicit (outcome, artifact, shortcut)
  • High curiosity without vagueness
  • Concrete nouns + power verbs in line one [5]
  • Borrowed credibility is attributed (tag if appropriate)
  • Based on a proven pattern—with your twist

Approved formats to remix

  • “X did Y the hard way. Here’s the short path for Z.”
  • “I tested X so you don’t have to. Here’s what works.”
  • “The overlooked law of [result].”
  • “If you struggle with [pain], use this.”

Common faults

  • Vague superlatives, jargon, hedging, stacked qualifiers

Drill: Five-Hook Sprint

  1. Write five variants for one idea (change noun/verb/object).
  2. Keep the clearest single benefit; delete the rest.
  3. Read aloud—pick the one with the best “music.”

B) Idea Selection with “Signal Fit”

Your idea must be both viral-capable and brand-relevant.

Signal Fit Filters

  • Relevance: ladders to offer/positioning
  • Novelty: fresh insight, artifact, or data
  • Emotion: hope, envy, or fear is clear
  • Evidence: ≥ 2 credible sources
  • Platform fit: travels cross-platform without breaking

Proofing Sources

  • Swipe vaults, platform search, client artifacts
  • Blend multiple sources to avoid “rehash” risk

C) Structure with 1–3–1

1 tight lead → 3 lines/steps of substance → 1 punchy close/transition. This mirrors scanner behavior; concise, chunked content outperforms walls of text [1][6].

Lead: “Your CTA fails because it sells the tool, not the win.”

  • Promise the outcome in plain words.
  • Show proof before the link.
  • Give one clear next step.

Close: “Do this and CTR climbs fast.”

D) Voice Matching & Client DNA

  • Extract tone, rhythm, lexical tics from prior posts/transcripts
  • Ask: “If the name were hidden, would followers know it’s them?”

Voice elements to record

  • Tone spectrum (serious…playful)
  • Sentence cadence & rhythm
  • Figures of speech & favorite verbs
  • Taboo words & default POV

Mini voice board (example)

“One lever. One metric. One win.” 
“Proof or pass.” 
“Ship by Friday.”

E) Clarity, Fluency, Readability

  • Prefer short words & concrete images
  • Swap stacks of prepositions for direct verbs
  • Remove hedges (“might,” “kind of,” “really”) unless for credibility
  • Front-load keywords for scanners [1][7]
  • Make terms/structure consistent (processing fluency) [2]

F) Authority without Plagiarism

  • Don’t lift text verbatim—remix structure; add unique analysis
  • Credit ideas; tag near the reference and again after the CTA

3) Platform Playbooks

X (Twitter)

Hook & Lead

  • Hook = benefit + curiosity
  • Lead tweet sets scope & primes the sequence

Body Rules

  • One idea per tweet
  • ≤ 9 lines per tweet for readability
  • Use clean bullets “•”
  • Put strongest insight early; vary cadence

Finish

Mic-drop; then a CTA tied to the post’s central benefit.

Example Thread Skeleton

1) Hook: “Add 10–15 demos/week with one habit.”
2) Law: “Proof beats polish.”
3) Steps: “Find, Fix, Feature.”
4) Case: “Jake: 0 → 12 demos in 9 days.”
5) CTA: “Copy the checklist. Link.”

LinkedIn

Open Strong

First 2 lines must carry the post; earn “see more.”

Formats that travel

  • Story → lesson
  • Framework cheatsheet
  • Resource list

Formatting & Media

  • Short paragraphs, bullets, Title Case headings
  • Carousels for frameworks/data
  • Soft CTA aligned to the promise

Long-Form, Email, UX

Long-Form

  • Wayfinding sections
  • “Do this next” boxes
  • Subheads that skim as an outline [1]

Email

  • Subject & preheader = benefit
  • One goal per send

UX Microcopy

  • Plain language; next-step guidance
  • No blame in errors; reduce friction [8]

4) Editing, QA, and the “Punch” Loop

Pass 1 — Clarity

  • Cut filler & adverbs
  • Collapse clauses
  • Front-load key terms

Pass 2 — Specificity

  • Replace generalities with numbers/names/artifacts
  • Ground every claim

Pass 3 — Rhythm

  • Vary length; remove drones
  • Add a short “stab” line where energy sags

QA Checklist

  • Hook tested against swipe
  • 1–3–1 cadence in each section
  • What/Why/How present
  • Client voice intact
  • Links full (with protocol) and secure
  • Locale & grammar pass complete

5) Media, Evidence, and Swipe Discipline

Use media when it shortens cognition

  • Celebrity breakdowns
  • Data/visual patterns
  • Before/after proof

Storage & credit

  • Central “Client Media” folder + link under post
  • License or permission documented
  • Consistent naming & tags
Swipe Vault Discipline: Tag by topic, format, emotion; save structure (not words); record the proof angle and CTA path that made it work.

6) Conversion Layer: Transitions & CTAs

Transitions that Feel Earned

Bridge from story insight to offer logic: audience → attention → authority → outcome. Keep it short. Layer persuasion ethically [3].

Transition Formula:

  1. Restate the story’s “why.”
  2. Tie to your capability → reader’s outcome.
  3. Add one proof line (number/timeframe).
  4. One clear next step.

CTA Rules

  • Sell the outcome, not the mechanism
  • One action. One link.
  • Inline credibility (count/result/qualifier)
  • FOMO/urgency only if real
  • Match brand voice & promise

Soft

“Want the full checklist? Grab it here.”

Medium

“Ready to ship this by Friday? Book a 15-min audit.”

Hard

“Need 10–15 demos/week this quarter? Apply here.”

7) Measurement & Iteration (what makes you lethal)

North-Star

Link clicks and clicks per 1,000 impressions.

What usually moves the needle

  • Idea quality (topic + hook)
  • CTA clarity & placement
  • Earned transition

Weekly loop

  1. Sort posts by link clicks.
  2. Label winning idea/transition/CTA traits.
  3. Ship 3 controlled variants (change one element).
  4. Track with UTMs; review down-funnel performance.

8) Rubrics, Checklists, Templates

Elite Thread Rubric (0–3 each; pass ≥ 20/24)

  • Hook clarity
  • Hook curiosity
  • Idea relevance
  • Concrete evidence
  • 1–3–1 cadence
  • Specificity & actionability
  • Voice fidelity
  • CTA strength

Universal Writer’s Checklist

  • Hook = concrete, curiosity-charged, benefit-forward
  • Idea passes Signal Fit
  • 1–3–1 everywhere
  • What/Why/How present
  • Sentences vary for musical rhythm
  • No fluff/hedges/clichés
  • Client voice recognizable
  • Links full & secure
  • Locale & grammar clean
  • Media sourced, credited, stored

Templates (paste-and-adapt)

Hook remixes

“The overlooked rule behind [result]: [specific artifact].”
“I studied [X exemplars]. Here are [Y] patterns you can steal.”
“[Pain] is optional. Use this to get [result].”

Story skeleton

Set stakes → show the turn → extract the law → apply it → bridge to offer.

Framework skeleton

9) Ethics & Risk

  • Cite when someone’s idea enables your point
  • No lifting hooks verbatim
  • No fabricated numbers/quotes/screenshots
  • No bait-and-switch CTAs—the brand is your credibility

10) Your Personal Upgrade Plan (deliberate practice)

Daily (15–20 min)

  • Rewrite one viral hook into 3 variants in client voice
  • Convert two long sentences into a short, punchy pair
  • Maintain a “Dead Words” list to avoid forever

Weekly (45–60 min)

  • Build/update a “Voice Board” (5 lines that define cadence)
  • Archive 10 swipe items; label hook type, proof, CTA path
  • Run a small A/B on hook or CTA (change one element)
Meta-drill (Gary Provost cadence): Write five consecutive short sentences for punch. Follow with one long sentence that rolls and rises to a controlled crest, then end with a one-beat line. That contrast sells.
