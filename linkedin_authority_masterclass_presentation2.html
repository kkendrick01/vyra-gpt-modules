DM Funnel

The 3-Message DM Sequence

  1. Message 1: Acknowledge, anchor to their context, ask one qualifying question.
  2. Message 2: Dig deeper on the problem. One or two sharp questions.
  3. Message 3: Invite to a quick call with a clear purpose and time window.

The goal is movement to a call with clarity, not endless chat.

VYRALAB
Slide 14 of 26
Diagnostic Call

The 20-Minute Framework

  • Minutes 0–3: Context and goals. Confirm why they booked.
  • Minutes 3–10: Problem excavation. Timeline, attempts, impact, cost of inaction.
  • Minutes 10–17: Mirror back. Outline 2–3 key steps you would use.
  • Minutes 17–20: Confirm fit and propose next steps.

You listen more than you talk. You lead with clarity, not pressure.

VYRALAB

Proposal

Simple, Clear, Closed

  1. Situation: 2–3 sentences summarizing their current state.
  2. Solution: Bullets with deliverables and timeline.
  3. Investment: Price and terms.
  4. Next Steps: Exact action to start.

A strong diagnostic call makes this a confirmation step.

Troubleshooting

Problem 1: No DMs

  • Check: Is your thesis specific enough?
  • Check: Are your posts talking to buyers or to peers?
  • Check: Is your CTA clear and direct?

Adjust thesis, sharpen hook, and use a concrete CTA that invites response.

Troubleshooting

Problem 2: DMs, No Calls

  • Tighten the 3-message sequence.
  • Ask one strong qualifying question early.
  • Offer a clear, low-friction call invite with a defined outcome.
