MODULE 0 — Operating Principles (Make Clients Successful On Purpose)

Outcome over output

  • Every artifact maps to a business goal the client stated.
  • Every week shows movement toward that goal on the MAP.
  • Every review ties feedback to the goal tree, not taste.

Frictionless by design

  • Async inputs preferred; multiple low-effort modes offered.
  • Approvals inside named surfaces with deadlines in view.
  • Decisions captured once in systems the client can see.

Proactive posture

  • Weekly status rhythm with next steps and owners.
  • Early blocker disclosure with options and impact.
  • Performance readouts translated into decisions.

Proof early, personalization next

  • Week 1–2: battle-tested patterns in client lane.
  • After first win: enable the Personalization Engine.
  • Personal content cadence agreed and visible in MAP.

MODULE 1 — The Customer Psychology Map (Why Clients Churn… or Stay)

Common fears

  • Wasted time and missed windows
  • Generic voice that dilutes brand
  • Silent teams and scope drift
  • “This looks nothing like me”

Retention drivers

  • Predictable progress and visible wins
  • Unmistakable brand voice
  • Clear calendar and decisive comms
  • Thoughtful pushback and easy approvals

Practice translation

  • Start on proven patterns to establish competence.
  • Layer Personalization Engine once trust exists.
  • Keep a public MAP with current status and next steps.

MODULE 2 — The Mutual Action Plan (MAP) Clients Can See

Use a single page the client can open at any time. Four rows cover the full lifecycle. The MAP removes ambiguity and compresses decisions.

RowPurposeExample Fields
Alignment Define goals and guardrails Goals • ICPs • Offers • Profile changes • Idea shortlist
Creation Track inputs and production Outlines requested • Assets received • Drafts queued
Approval Clear bottlenecks fast Awaiting review • Approved • Blocked with reason
Launch & Learn Close the loop with data Calendar • Posted links • Results • Changes queued
Rule: Nothing important lives in DMs. If it matters, it lives on the MAP.

MODULE 3 — Pre-Onboarding Intelligence Pack

Build these assets before the first call. The client experiences clarity on day one.

Core contents

  • Goal Tree (client words): primary outcome with supporting outcomes
  • Offer/CTA stack: primary and backup CTAs aligned to transitions
  • Audience & proof: ICP snapshots, credibility assets, artifacts
  • Idea shortlist (2–4 wks): viral pattern + relevance note + CTA path
  • Profile optimization draft: bios, banner notes, featured links
  • Launch plan: week-by-week milestones

Pre-Onboarding message (outline)

Agenda → goals/strategy, workflow, idea review, profile updates.
Share-links → shortlist + banner/bio options.
Outcome → approve a Launch Plan on the call.

MODULE 4 — Onboarding Call Mastery (Run It Like a Pro)

Opening line

“Our job is to make this effortless and effective. We’ll handle the heavy lifting and keep you fully in the loop. Today we align goals, finalize direction, confirm workflow, and leave with a Launch Plan you approve.”

Workflow alignment

  • Posting days and cross-posting plan
  • Publisher of record and approval path
  • Home for outlines/media and involvement level

Goals → Strategy

  • Confirm business outcome in client words
  • Choose breadth vs. niche for Month 1
  • Lock the primary CTA

Personal Brand Blueprint

  • Origin arc and signature beliefs
  • Contrarians and taboo lines
  • Tone sliders and exact phrases

Idea review

  • State why each idea wins in this lane
  • Show the offer link before writing
  • Point to the client fingerprint planned

Close

  • Recap decisions and approvals
  • Confirm deliverables and inputs
  • Point to the MAP; get a verbal “yes” on Launch Plan

MODULE 5 — The Idea Gate (Zero “Meh” Allowed)

Pass criteria

  • Viral pattern proof exists with precedent
  • Relevance line to offer/authority is obvious
  • Emotion present without clickbait
  • Artifact ready: screenshots, charts, quotes, media
  • CTA defined now

Reject politely or reframe until it passes. Document the decision inside the MAP.

MODULE 6 — The Personalization Engine (The Retention Lever)

Deploy when the client lacks a content bank or when voice depth is required. The system captures truth with minimal effort on the client side.

Outline System

  • Client supplies short outlines on approved ideas
  • Accept via Typefully, Slack, Looms, voice notes
  • AM transcribes and labels as Personal Idea
  • Assets tagged in Drive and linked on the MAP

Why it works

Personal truth inside proven structure creates sticky authority and defensible voice. The cadence compounds retention.

MODULE 7 — Draft Gate & Editorial Standards

Hook & Structure

  • Hook shows curiosity with specificity
  • 1–3–1 rhythm visible
  • Short/medium/long sentence music present

Substance

  • What / Why / How in each point
  • Benefits over mechanisms
  • Transitions feel earned

Compliance

  • Client voice unmistakable
  • Full links with protocol
  • Imagery sourced and filed
  • Locale and grammar clean

MODULE 8 — Communication System (Never Get “Any Update?” Again)

Standards

  • Same-day acknowledgment for every client message
  • Weekly status recap: shipped, next, needs
  • Every reply includes owner and next step
  • Early blocker disclosure with options
  • Tone: crisp, respectful, decisive

Slack snippets

Acknowledgment + path
“Got this. We’ll incorporate the two points on authority tone and swap the CTA to the newsletter. Updated timeline lives on your MAP.”

Blocker disclosure
“We’re paused on Post #3 until we receive the screenshot. Options:
a) publish #4 in its slot, b) replace #3 with a proven alternative,
c) hold the slot for one cycle. Preference?”

  • Publish with tight transitions to one clear CTA
  • Show visible proof: screens, quotes, artifacts
  • After wins, enable the Personalization Engine cadence

Credibility leads to engagement. Engagement enriches outlines. Drafts sharpen. Results accelerate.

MODULE 11 — Health Scoring, Risk Radar, and Save-Plays

Green

  • On-time approvals
  • Early visible wins
  • Responsive comms

Yellow

  • Slow approvals
  • Recurring “off-voice” comments
  • Idea fatigue

Red

  • Missed launches
  • Silent sponsor
  • Scope confusion
  • Negative surprise

Save-Plan Script

  1. Name reality: what we see and the impact on their goal
  2. Offer options: idea mix, approval path, CTA change, media support
  3. Gain commitments: owners and dates on both sides
  4. Short-cycle a win: ship one high-confidence post

Escalations are handled live and then recorded on the MAP.

MODULE 12 — Objections & Hard Moments (Exact Language)

Hands-off + hyper-personal

“Strong results require little effort from you using proven ideas. For personal pieces, a brief voice note or outline unlocks your unique stories. We make it painless and do the lifting.”

“Why aren’t we viral?”

“We optimize for repeatable wins. Here’s what compounds now: [proof]. Next, we widen reach with [pattern] while holding clicks through [CTA refinement].”

“This doesn’t sound like me.”

“Share three lines that feel perfectly you. We’ll bake them into your style guide and tune every draft to match.”

Scope drift

“Happy to help. This sits outside scope. Two paths: swap a planned item or add this as a paid add-on. Which keeps you moving best?”

MODULE 13 — Renewal & Expansion (QBR Without the Corporate Yawn)

Quarterly Review Framework

  • Outcome recap vs. goal
  • Signal deep-dive: ideas, transitions, CTAs that printed
  • Voice evolution in market
  • Opportunity board with projected impact
  • Decision ask: renew, upgrade cadence, or add a lane

Advocacy flywheel

Win → artifact → micro-case → testimonial or cameo → referral ask. Provide scripts and easy accept paths.

MODULE 14 — Governance & QA (No Surprises)

  • Idea Gate before writing
  • Draft Gate before client review
  • Calendar Gate before publishing

If a piece is good and not great, recycle the angle. The standard protects the brand.

No-Plagiarism Guardrails

  • No word-for-word hooks or bodies
  • Pull from multiple sources; tag and credit when appropriate
  • Add original analysis and proof

Drive

Slack

MODULE 17 — Advanced: Personalization at Scale (Without Burning the Client)

Voice seeds

Collect 20 phrases that sound quintessentially the client. Sprinkle across drafts to maintain identity under speed.

Proof library

Screenshots, charts, quotes, customer notes organized by topic and pillar. Link from the MAP and Typefully cards.

Transition bank

A dozen transition styles with swappable benefit lines per offer. Keep Soft/Medium/Hard CTA variants pre-approved in a matrix.

MODULE 18 — What Great Looks Like (Acceptance Criteria)

  • Client can repeat the Launch Plan without notes
  • First visible win captured and showcased
  • Voice lock-in after first feedback cycle
  • Calendar certainty with no surprise gaps
  • Proactive stance visible in status and options
  • MAP hygiene with nothing important outside it

MODULE 19 — Day-to-Day Running Order (Sequence Only)

  1. Check MAP for decisions and blockers.
  2. Review client messages; acknowledge and assign owners.
  3. Idea Gate on new items; move approved to Ready to Write.
  4. Drafts → Draft Gate → client review.
  5. Approvals → schedule → post → capture proof to library.
  6. Update MAP; post weekly recap; queue next cycle.

MODULE 20 — Culture of Candor (The Retention Multiplier)

Tell the truth fast

If something slipped, state it, own it, fix it, and document the prevention step inside the MAP.

Educate the why

Translate tradeoffs into simple choices with impact and timelines. Confident clients renew.

Make advocacy easy

After wins, provide a one-paragraph testimonial prompt and a simple referral script.
